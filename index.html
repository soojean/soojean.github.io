Welcome to my website!

I am a postdoctoral scholar with the Autonomous Robotics and Control Lab at Caltech. I completed my Ph.D. in Control and Dynamical Systems under the supervision of Professor John C. Doyle and Professor Soon-Jo Chung. My thesis, entitled Control and State-Estimation of Jump Stochastic Systems by Learning Recurrent Spatiotemporal Patterns, can be read here. Prior to that, I received my B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science and Applied Mathematics at UC Berkeley in Fall 2016.

My PhD Defense [Jan 2023]

-----

My research broadly deals with efficient control for non-Gaussian stochastic systems which have jump phenomena, such as Poisson shot noise perturbations and hybrid dynamical systems. My research targets applications in large-scale network systems, fault-tolerance, and aerospace robotics (e.g., swarm intelligence) where systems are often modeled with recurrent jump patterns. I am also interested in learning-based estimation and control algorithms that take advantage of a system's patterns over space (e.g., topological symmetries) and over time, such as distributed network control and graph convolutional neural networks.
